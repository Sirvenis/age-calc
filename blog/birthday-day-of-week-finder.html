What Day of the Week Were You Born On?

Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 5 min

Have you ever wondered what day of the week you were born on? It's a fun fact that reveals interesting patterns about dates and time. And yes, you can calculate it without looking up old records.

The Doomsday Algorithm

Mathematician John Conway created the "Doomsday" method for mentally calculating the day of the week for any date. The key insight: certain dates always fall on the same day of the week within a given year.

Doomsday dates include: 4/4, 6/6, 8/8, 10/10, 12/12, and the last day of February. Once you know what day the "doomsday" falls on, you can calculate any other date.

Fun Facts About Birthdays

  • Tuesday is the most common day for births (statistically)
  • Sunday is the least common (fewer scheduled deliveries)
  • About 1 in 1,461 people are born on February 29
  • The most common birthday is September 9, least common is December 25

Your Birthday Pattern

Because a year is 52 weeks plus 1 day (or 2 in a leap year), your birthday shifts forward by one day each year. If your birthday is on a Monday this year, it'll be on a Tuesday next year. Leap years add an extra day shift for birthdays after February 28.
